## 1. What I Found: The "Train-Serve Skew" Demon
While investigating the discrepancy between excellent training results and abysmal inference/backtest performance, I discovered a critical **Train-Serve Skew (Lookahead/Scale Bias)** issue. 

During the Phase 11c architecture update (`INS-049` / `INS-051`), the training pipeline was fundamentally altered:
* **The Change:** All cross-batch normalization (Softmax/Z-score) was abolished to prevent "Macro Beta Smuggling". 
* **The New Standard:** The model (`train.py` / `compute_spear_loss`) was upgraded to output **absolute Basis Points (BP)** directly, using Pointwise Huber Loss. 

However, the inference and backtesting scripts were **never updated** to reflect this architectural shift. They still assumed the model's output was Z-score normalized and forcefully applied a reverse scaling operation:
```python
# The lethal legacy code:
pred_bp = (preds.squeeze() * TARGET_STD + TARGET_MEAN).numpy().copy()
```
Since the model was already outputting absolute BP (e.g., `20 BP`), this reverse scaling multiplied the output by `TARGET_STD` (216.24), resulting in **catastrophic scale explosions** (e.g., 20 BP became 4319 BP). This entirely destroyed the mathematical validity of the backtesting engine and any downstream portfolio allocation logic that relied on variance or Softmax.

## 2. Secondary Finding: The Ghost of Bug C-008
In the same line of code, the legacy `.squeeze()` operation was still being used. As documented in `tests/test_known_bugs.py`, `.squeeze()` on a `[1]`-shaped tensor causes a dimension collapse to a scalar `[]`, which breaks downstream `.tolist()` or NumPy array indexing operations.

## 3. What I Did & Why

I synchronized the inference and backtesting engines with the Phase 11c absolute BP standard, and simultaneously patched the C-008 dimension collapse vulnerability.

### 3.1. Removing Reverse Scaling (TARGET_STD / TARGET_MEAN)
**Why:** The model natively predicts absolute BP. Scaling it by historical STD/MEAN creates massive distortions.
**Action:** Stripped out the `* TARGET_STD + TARGET_MEAN` operations from all inference and backtesting scripts.

### 3.2. Patching C-008 Dimension Collapse
**Why:** To ensure stability when processing single samples or trailing batches of size 1.
**Action:** Replaced all vulnerable `.squeeze()` calls on predictions with the safe `.view(-1)` operation.

### 3.3. Fixing Tensorboard Variance Contamination
**Why:** The training loop's logging also incorrectly scaled the standard deviation of predictions, polluting Tensorboard metrics with artificial scale expansion.
**Action:** Removed `* TARGET_STD` from the `pred_std_bp` calculation in the validation loops.
